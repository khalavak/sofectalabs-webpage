Cutting Through the Noise: How SofectaLabs is Revolutionizing Cybersecurity with AI

Using AI-powered triage and automation to filter the overwhelming volume of security alerts.

Cybersecurity threats have never been more sophisticated or relentless. Even the most advanced security systems struggle to keep up with the overwhelming volume of alerts. At SofectaLabs, our internal dashboard lights up with over a thousand cybersecurity alarms daily, requiring swift and accurate triage. Shockingly, more than 90% of these alerts are false positives.

The challenge: Fighting through the noise

Security teams spend 32% of their day chasing false alarms, leading to analyst fatigue and delayed responses to real cyberattacks. How do we cut through the noise and focus on genuine threats?

The solution: AI-powered triage and automation

At SofectaLabs, we've developed an AI-driven case analysis and triage system that acts as an intelligent filter. Using the latest LLM models, our AI:

  • Distinguishes real threats from false positives
  • Automates routine case analysis and triage
  • Enables near real-time threat detection
"Studies show that AI-driven cybersecurity can reduce response times by 84% and improve threat detection accuracy by 60%."

AI automation flow: The ultimate force multiplier

  1. Ingest: Alerts from SOAR along with linked cases.
  2. Search: Historical cases via semantic search.
  3. Analyze: Determine severity and recommend next steps.
  4. Inject: AI reports into SOAR for analyst insight.
  5. Automate: Close low/medium-risk cases with high confidence.
  6. Route: High-risk cases to human analysts.

The tech powering our AI flow

Semantic search with Elasticsearch: Identifying conceptually similar cases even if keywords differ.

Flexible AI framework with LangGraph: A model-agnostic framework allowing us to integrate any leading LLM.

Why this matters

By augmenting human expertise with AI-driven automation, SofectaLabs is setting a new standard. Our intelligent triage engine accelerates response times and frees analysts from chasing false alarms—so they can focus on stopping real cyber threats.
