            <p>
                In today's interconnected manufacturing environment, IT operations and security play a crucial role in maintaining productivity and safety. However, several common mistakes continue to expose organizations to significant risks.
            </p>

            <h3>7 Common Pitfalls</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Outdated OS:</strong> Many facilities run critical machinery on deprecated systems like Windows XP, lacking security patches.</li>
                <li><strong>Insufficient Segmentation:</strong> Failing to properly isolate OT from IT networks allows threats to move laterally.</li>
                <li><strong>Inadequate Backups:</strong> Lack of tested disaster recovery plans for critical system configurations.</li>
                <li><strong>Poor Access Control:</strong> Shared accounts and weak password policies for industrial control systems.</li>
                <li><strong>Untrained Staff:</strong> Underestimating the importance of cybersecurity awareness among shop floor employees.</li>
                <li><strong>IoT Insecurity:</strong> Connected devices often have default passwords and lack regular monitoring.</li>
                <li><strong>Limited Monitoring:</strong> Absence of real-time threat detection and inadequate logging.</li>
            </ul>

            <blockquote>
                "Failing to segment operational technology (OT) from IT networks creates unnecessary risk exposure across the entire organization."
            </blockquote>

            <h3>Recommendations</h3>
            <p>
                To mitigate these risks, organizations should prioritize assets inventory, implement proper network segmentation, and partner with managed security providers for 24/7 monitoring.
            </p>
