            <p>
                Ransomware attacks increased by 93% in 2023 alone. Traditional SOCs are struggling to keep up with the scale and speed of modern threats. Artificial Intelligence, Large Language Models (LLMs), and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) are becoming game-changers for proactive mitigation.
            </p>

            <h3>SOC Challenges & AI Solutions</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Alert Fatigue:</strong> AI ranks alerts based on risk level, cutting through noise and helping teams focus on urgent threats.</li>
                <li><strong>Talent Shortage:</strong> AI automates routine tasks, allowing junior analysts to take on advanced threat assessments.</li>
                <li><strong>APTs:</strong> Establishing behavioral baselines allows for instant anomaly detection compared to threat intelligence feeds.</li>
                <li><strong>Complex Integration:</strong> AI-driven orchestration connects scattered data from multiple platforms into a clearer view.</li>
            </ul>

            <h3>Enhancing SOC Workflows</h3>
            <p>
                Integrating AI into SOC workflows improves efficiency via:
            </p>
            <ul>
                <li>Automated data analysis and real-time classification.</li>
                <li>Generative AI for summarizing emerging threats.</li>
                <li>RAG-based retrieval of structured security data.</li>
                <li>Automated remediation like IPs blocking or device isolation.</li>
            </ul>

            <blockquote>
                "AI should assist, not replace, human expertise. A hybrid approach ensures both efficiency and reliability."
            </blockquote>

            <h3>Responsible Implementation</h3>
            <p>
                Without human oversight, AI can introduce risks like adversarial attacks. At Sofecta Labs, we keep human expertise at the core, using AI to handle repetitive tasks while analysts make the critical decisions.
            </p>
