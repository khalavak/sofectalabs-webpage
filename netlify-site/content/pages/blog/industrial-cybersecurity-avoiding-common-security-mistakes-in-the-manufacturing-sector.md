            <h2>Increasing Risk</h2>
            <p>
                The manufacturing sector is an increasingly attractive target for cybercriminals. As factories become more connected and digitalized, they also become more vulnerable to the types of cyberattacks that were previously the domain of IT environments.
            </p>

            <h2>Common Mistakes</h2>
            <p>
                Many manufacturing organizations are making common mistakes that increase their risk of a cyberattack, including:
            </p>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Inadequate Segmentation:</strong> Failing to properly segment the OT network from the IT network.</li>
                <li><strong>Lack of Visibility:</strong> Having little or no visibility into the OT environment.</li>
                <li><strong>Poor Patch Management:</strong> Failing to keep OT systems and software up-to-date.</li>
            </ul>

            <h2>The Impact of Downtime</h2>
            <p>
                The impact of a cyberattack on a manufacturing organization can be devastating, leading to production downtime, financial loss, and reputational damage. In a sector where "uptime" is critical, even a minor incident can have significant consequences.
            </p>

            <blockquote>
                "Protecting your manufacturing environment requires a specialized approach that understands the unique needs and challenges of operational technology."
            </blockquote>
