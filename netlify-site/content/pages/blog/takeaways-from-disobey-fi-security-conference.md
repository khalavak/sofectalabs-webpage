            <p>
                Disobey Security Conference 2025, held at Kaapelitehdas in Helsinki, featured a diverse range of topics with a significant emphasis on ransomware and its escalating threat.
            </p>

            <h3>Ransomware Focus</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Hypervisor Protection:</strong> Strategies for safeguarding VMware ESXi servers.</li>
                <li><strong>Legacy Struggle:</strong> Detection and response within environments reliant on outdated infrastructure.</li>
                <li><strong>Weaponized Leaks:</strong> Exploring how leaked files can be used for automated threat intelligence against further victims.</li>
            </ul>

            <h3>"Old is New"</h3>
            <p>
                A recurring theme was that cybersecurity issues from two decades ago persist today. Single-bit errors in domain names can still expose OAuth tokens and emails, demonstrated by sessions from prominent Nordic hackers.
            </p>

            <h3>AI in the Hacker Scene</h3>
            <p>
                Artificial Intelligence was a prominent topic. Patria highlighted using LLMs for analyzing news feeds and translating content for military operations. Sofecta Labs has deployed similar solutions, using LLMs to analyze vulnerability information from multiple sources for swift response.
            </p>

            <blockquote>
                "Disobey continues to maintain its unique character as a hacker-friendly conference that doesn't shy away from controversial topics."
            </blockquote>

            <p>
                The mix of technical deep-dives and broader strategic discussions provided valuable insights for security practitioners and organizational leaders alike. We look forward to seeing how these insights shape the security landscape in the coming year.
            </p>
