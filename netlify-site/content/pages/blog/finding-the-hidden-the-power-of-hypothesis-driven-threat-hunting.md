            <h2>Beyond Alerts</h2>
            <p>
                In a modern SOC, alerts are constant. However, many of the most dangerous threats don't trigger alerts—they hide in the noise, slowly working toward their objectives. At Sofecta Labs, we use hypothesis-driven threat hunting to find these hidden threats.
            </p>

            <h2>The Power of a Hypothesis</h2>
            <p>
                Hypothesis-driven threat hunting starts with a question: "If an attacker was in our network using [specific technique], how would we know?" By developing a hypothesis based on real-world threat intelligence and known adversarial behaviors, we can search for the subtle indicators of compromise that others might miss.
            </p>

            <h2>Leveraging the MITRE ATT&CK Framework</h2>
            <p>
                Our hypotheses are informed by the MITRE ATT&CK framework, which provides a comprehensive knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques. By mapping our hunts to specific ATT&CK techniques, we can better understand the threats we face and develop more effective detection rules.
            </p>

            <blockquote>
                "Threat hunting is not about finding the needle in the haystack, but about knowing exactly where to look for it."
            </blockquote>

            <h2>Proactive Defense</h2>
            <p>
                By searching for anomalies and unusual activity, we can identify and neutralize threats before they can achieve their goals. This proactive approach not only improves security but also provides a range of business benefits, including reduced risk and improved operational resilience.
            </p>
